# Order of Review (OOR) — Quick Reference

This skill's classification follows **Supplement No. 4 to Part 774** (Commerce Control List Order of Review) and the "specially designed" definition in **15 CFR 772.1**. The methodology mirrors the BIS CCL Order of Review and Specially Designed decision tools.

## Jurisdiction first

- If the item is **described in the USML (22 CFR Part 121)**, including catch-all paragraphs, it is a defense article under ITAR. Do **not** run the CCL Order of Review; classify under the applicable USML category (DDTC).
- Only items **not** on the USML and otherwise "subject to the EAR" proceed through the CCL steps below.

## CCL Order of Review (Supplement No. 4 to Part 774)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **1** | General characteristics → identify CCL **category (0–9)**. |
| **2** | Determine **product group (A–E)** within that category. |
| **3** | Is the item in a **9x515 or 600 series** ECCN paragraph (other than catch-all)? If yes → classify there and stop. (These trump other CCL entries.) |
| **4** | If not Step 3: Is it in a 9x515/600 series **catch-all** (e.g. ".x" parts "specially designed" for that ECCN)? **4.a** Apply 15 CFR 772.1 (a)(1)/(a)(2) (catch). **4.b** If caught, apply 772.1 paragraph (b) (release). Only apply release when the ECCN uses "specially designed" as a **control** parameter. |
| **5** | Search the **rest of the CCL** (start of product group). Apply 4.a/4.b whenever an ECCN uses "specially designed." |
| **6** | Not in any ECCN → **EAR99**. (EAR99 may still need a license for end use/end user/destination.) |

## Ordering rules

- **600 series and 9x515** take precedence over other ECCNs.
- When multiple non-600/non-9x515 ECCNs could apply (e.g. 1A004 and 1A995): if the item has **civilian / non-military / non-CBRN** indications (e.g. civilian, industrial, commercial, consumer, safety equipment, protective equipment, environmental), **prefer 1A995 over 1A004**. Otherwise use the most specific or first applicable ECCN in product group order.
